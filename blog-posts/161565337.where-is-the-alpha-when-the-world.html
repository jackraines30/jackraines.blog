There’s two ways, really: the first way comes from asking good questions. If all of the data is accessible, how do you, dear reader, draw it from the abyss of trillions of potential things that could be written about?

The second thing is finding the sources that it can’t, whether behind paywalls or offline in total. In a way, it’s the inversion of what we saw in the 2000s.

Alpha used to be found on the internet. Now, the AI can search exponentially better and more effectively than you can. The alpha comes from how you ask the AI, or, more importantly, what you can find that the internet wouldn’t have in the first place.
