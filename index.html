Hello, internet stranger. If you found your way here, I assume you're at least somewhat tangentially interested in me, what I've written, or what I think. So I'll tell you a bit about all of the above. But before you go any further, I recommend you check out my book, Young Money.

book (not Jack)

I would loosely describe it as "life advice for 20-somethings who still want to live a little while they're young," or "a more fun Defining Decade." Your 20s are this weird, stressful, seemingly-always-high-stakes period of life. They're also incredibly fun. So I wrote a book on how to make the most of that weird, stressful, fun period. If you, like me, subjected yourself to occasional (or frequent) bouts of stress and overthinking about making the most of "the current moment," you'll probably like this book.

About Me (in 25 seconds)

I'm an investor, writer https://www.youngmoney.co/, author https://www.amazon.com/Young-Money-Wealth-Purpose-Twenties/dp/B0FZS5PL15/ref=tmm_hrd_swatch_0, occasional Linkedin shitposter https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jackraines_beermile-sigmagrindset-iputthegoldingoldman-activity-7054462511835705345-fVVJ, former vagabond traveler https://www.youngmoney.co/p/pre-travel-check, occasional markets speculator https://www.youngmoney.co/p/gone-sixty-seconds?utm_source=publication-search, Twitter addict https://x.com/Jack_Raines, mediocre Spanish speaker, Citibike connoisseur, New York City fanatic, business school apologist, and proud personality hire in most aspects of life.

I'm 28 (as of March 2026), I live in New York (best city in the world), I spend a lot of time in San Francisco, and I'm from South Georgia. I speak Spanish with the fluency of a seven-year-old Guatemalan child. I had a quarter-life crisis at the age of 24 that took me to something like ~20 countries in a year (said crisis is mentioned in the above-mentioned book), including a three-month stint in Argentina, which I quite liked. Great food and awful currency there, let me tell you.

I'm a self-defined life enthusiast. I think it's important to be enthusiastic about life; there's too much going on to be bored or apathetic.

I work for an early-stage venture capital fund called Slow Ventures, and split my time across our core seed fund and our creator fund, meaning I split my time between talking to tech wizards building AI god and YouTube savants building, well, a lot of weird stuff. If you're building something interesting and want to take some expensive money from us, shoot me an email at jack@slow.co.
